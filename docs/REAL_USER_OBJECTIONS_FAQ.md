## 1. "I can just call the bank or file a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in. Why do I need your app?"

**Real answer:**

**Start with the official GRM (Grievance Redressal Mechanism) + NCRP/1930.** GRM (strengthened 2026 by I4C) is the dedicated free primary path for unfreezing wrongly frozen/lien accounts — direct bank + police coordination, video verification, time-bound accountability (e.g., bank review ~7d, IO ~15d). MRM handles victim fund restoration. Real results already documented (Mumbai examples). Entry: cybercrime.gov.in (GRM sections) or 1930.

Unhold is **not** a replacement. It is the best supplementary preparation layer: AI turns your notice into GRM-relevant clarity, builds matched checklists + verified bundles + GRM-optimized letters, and tracks your official steps so your GRM submission is the strongest possible.

Calling the bank helpline (or filing on NCRP at cybercrime.gov.in / dialing 1930) is the correct **entry step**. It creates an official record.

However, according to the 2026 I4C SOP and widespread user reports:

- The bank usually **freezes/holds** based on a police or cyber cell instruction. GRM is the structured review path.
- The Investigating Officer (IO) has up to 15 calendar days to decide (per 2026 SOP). The bank must review within 7 days.
- In practice, many people report:
  - "Complaint closed" but account still frozen weeks/months later.
  - Zero communication from branch.
  - Multiple visits needed with no movement.
  - Cases lasting 2–19+ months (documented examples include students and small traders frozen over tiny UPI amounts like ₹2–₹10,000 from traced fraud chains). Real example (2026 Reddit r/bangalore): user received ₹2 unknown UPI linked to fraud → entire ₹1800 balance frozen; local police demanded ₹10,000 "to unfreeze"; lawyer wanted ₹7k. User (student) could not afford and had to drop it. Many report the same micro-amount trigger + full freeze despite only disputed ₹2.

**What actually moves cases forward** for innocent holders:
- Submitting a **GRM-optimized written representation** with full documents (statements, source of funds proof, transaction details, explanation letter) directly via GRM portal + to the cyber cell/IO and the bank nodal officer.
- Creating a clear paper trail of follow-ups.
- Escalating when timelines are missed (new SOP has automatic escalation to District Grievance Officer).

A single phone call or basic NCRP filing often gets lost in the system. A well-organized, timestamped, GRM-prepped submission package carries more weight. Unhold helps you produce exactly that.

**Specific to tiny unknown amounts (₹2 example):** This is extremely common. Fraud rings send micro amounts to test accounts or lengthen trails. You (layer 3+) are often 100% innocent with no knowledge or relationship. Full account freeze happens anyway. GRM exists for review. Prepare: (1) full bank statement showing the tiny credit + all your normal salary/other activity, (2) simple written declaration "I do not know the sender, no prior contact, funds not from any fraud I participated in", (3) PAN/ID, (4) freeze notice. Sealed bundle + formal letter to nodal + GRM submission is what moves these.

From full Reddit/X comments (real users in same situation):
- "Write an email to the grievance officer of the bank. If they don't respond, write to the RBI Ombudsman. Don't give anyone a single rupee."
- Many on 5th layer told "only lawyer can solve" — but free escalation paths exist.
- RBI guideline repeatedly cited: Only the disputed amount (the ₹2) should be held, not the entire account.
- Practical: Delink phone from UPI to block unknown sends going forward.
- Unhold helps by giving you the professional package (bundle + letter) to attach to that first grievance email to the bank. Then track and escalate with proof if needed. This matches Bank of Baroda steps (identify reason → docs → contact bank) + official 2026 SOP GRM process.

**How Unhold helps here** (across many scenarios from 2026 research):
- Guided intake captures the exact details police/banks/GRM ask for (role, recognize funds?, NCRP, amount).
- Evidence is SHA-256 verified + AI-checked (with visible feedback).
- You can generate a **sealed evidence bundle** (PDF with manifest) — a single professional artifact instead of loose screenshots.
- For long delays/layer cases/P2P: Analyzer explains specific pattern + GRM path. Checklist tailors proofs (statements for normal activity, source for trade, timeline for Ombudsman). Bundle + letters for grievance email / IO / RBI. Tracker for 7d/15d deadlines. Real stories (₹2 student, 19mo case, trader ₹12L over ₹15k, no-FIR HC wins) show documented package + escalation moves cases when plain complaints fail.
- Templates and tracking for proper escalation letters (L1 to nodal level, GRM-tuned) with proof of sending.
- Full audit log of everything you did.

You still do the sending and the work through official channels (GRM primary). Unhold just makes the package stronger and the process less chaotic when you're stressed and your money is locked.

**Sources**: I4C SOP 2026 (7-day bank review, 15-day IO decision, grievance ladder + new GRM/MRM mechanisms), multiple 2025-2026 user reports on X and Reddit, Outlook Money case of 19-month freeze resolved only after RBI Ombudsman intervention.

## 2. "My friend just called the branch and it got released in 3 days. Why bother with letters and apps?"

**Real answer:**

This happens — especially in very clean cases where:
- The amount is tiny.
- The freeze was a low-level automatic flag.
- The branch manager was cooperative that day.
- The investigation quickly confirmed no involvement.

**The problem**: This is not reliable or common for many innocent receivers (especially "Layer 3" or further in money trails).

Real experiences (2025–2026):
- People report calling repeatedly with no update.
- "KYC done, home verification done, still frozen after 1–2 months."
- Complaint numbers closed but lien remains.
- Students and salaried people borrowing money while salary sits frozen.

When the fraud trail is complex or the police station is overloaded, a casual call is not enough. Banks are bound by the order from the enforcement agency. They need clearance or strong documentation from you.

Formal written escalation + documented evidence creates accountability and a record you can show to higher authorities (nodal officer, Ombudsman, or court if needed).

Unhold is insurance for the cases that **don't** resolve with one phone call.

## 3. "Just file an FIR or go to the police station yourself. Why letters?"

**Real answer:**

For genuine account holders, the standard path is often:
1. Report on NCRP / 1930 (already done in many cases by the original victim).
2. Approach the concerned cyber cell / IO with a written explanation + documents requesting NOC (No Objection Certificate) or release.
3. Follow up in writing if no response within SOP timelines.
4. Escalate to bank nodal + RBI Ombudsman if needed.

Visiting the police station helps, but they are often busy with the main fraud case. A clear, professional written submission (with verified evidence) is what many guides and successful users recommend sending via email/post to the IO.

Unhold helps you prepare that written package cleanly (bundle + letter draft + proof of prior steps).

Unhold makes the "proactive" work organized and professional instead of scattered WhatsApp messages and loose printouts.

## 5. "I already have my NCRP number and FIR. What more do I need?"

**Real answer:**

NCRP number is important — it proves you reported or are aware.

What banks and cyber cells typically still ask for from innocent holders:
- Last 6 months bank statements showing pattern of legitimate transactions.
- Proof of source of funds (salary slips, client invoices, family transfer proof, etc.).
- Explanation letter: who sent the money, why, your relationship, screenshots of chats/calls if any.
- KYC documents.
- Sometimes specific transaction details with UTRs.

A sealed, timestamped bundle containing all this + a formal escalation letter to the right level is stronger than just saying "I have NCRP number 12345."

Unhold's Evidence Bundle + letter drafts are designed exactly for this submission package.

## 12. "If I paste my story into Claude (Opus 4.8) or ChatGPT with a good prompt, it can write the emails and letters to the bank/police. Why use Unhold?"

**Real answer (be direct and detailed):**

A general LLM like Claude or GPT can generate text for emails and letters if you give it a long, detailed prompt. That is true.

Here is exactly why that is usually not enough for this problem, and why Unhold is built differently:

1. **One-off text vs a complete professional package**  
   LLM gives you words. Unhold gives you:
   - A single **sealed evidence bundle PDF** (cover page + full manifest with SHA-256 hashes of every file). This is a verifiable artifact you can actually hand to the bank or cyber cell. A ChatGPT email with attached random screenshots is much weaker.

2. **Document verification (not just generation)**  
   When you upload files to Unhold, they get SHA-256 hashed and run through AI analysis (NVIDIA). You get live feedback on authenticity, mismatches, and flags. The system records this.  
   A general LLM cannot look at your actual bank statement PDF and tell you "this amount looks inconsistent with your narration" or create a cryptographically sealed version.

3. **Enforced escalation ladder + proof gates**  
   In real cases, you often need L1 first, then prove you sent it (screenshot or acknowledgement), then L2.  
   Unhold has built-in proof gates: you cannot (in the system) move to the next level without uploading proof. This creates a clean, defensible timeline.  
   With Claude you have to manually remember the order, track what you sent when, and hope you did it right. Many people mess this up and cases drag.

4. **Persistent, credible audit trail**  
   Unhold keeps a full machine-recorded history: every upload, every letter generated, every action you marked, with timestamps. This record lives in the case and can be shown to the bank, police, or RBI Ombudsman.  
   A conversation in ChatGPT is just chat history that anyone can question ("did you really send that?").

5. **Domain-specific structure, not generic advice**  
   Unhold uses actual SBI playbooks, innocent-receiver templates, and the exact escalation paths that work in practice for UPI freeze cases.  
   A general LLM (even with a long prompt) can hallucinate the wrong officer, wrong format, wrong wording, or suggest things that don't help (or even hurt) in these specific cases. It has no persistent memory of your exact case across sessions.

6. **Real-time help + tracking while stressed**  
   When your money is frozen, you are not in the best state to write perfect multi-turn prompts and manage 15 different chat threads.  
   Unhold gives guided questions, shows you what is still missing, creates next steps automatically, and reminds you. It reduces mistakes that come from being overwhelmed.

**Bottom line**:
Claude/ChatGPT can help you write text. That is useful for many things.  
For a frozen bank account where you need to prove innocence to an institution that moves slowly, what usually works is:
- Verified + sealed evidence (not loose files)
- Proper multi-level escalation with proof at each step
- A clean, timestamped record of everything

Unhold is purpose-built to produce exactly that package, following the real process (including the 2026 SOP timelines and grievance path). A general LLM prompt does not replace the system that enforces structure, verification, and proof.

You can still use Claude to review the drafts Unhold generates if you want. Many people will. But the tool is there to create something stronger and more organized than raw LLM output.

## 13. "Why don't you just make the product send the emails and letters automatically? Then the user only needs to check responses."

**Real answer (this is non-negotiable):**

We deliberately **do not and will not** add auto-send.

Reasons:

- **Hard rule in the spec**: BUILD_SPEC.md explicitly forbids `send_email`, auto-send, `file_rbi`, `file_ncrp`, etc. Letters are "copy-only". There's even a CI script (`scripts/verify-no-auto-send.sh`) that fails the build if any auto-send code appears.

- **Legal & liability risk**: If the app auto-sends a letter on the user's behalf and something goes wrong (wrong wording, wrong timing, the bank reacts badly, or the user later says "I didn't authorize that"), the user can get in serious trouble with the bank, police, or legally. We are not lawyers and we are not authorized to act on behalf of the user.

- **Trust & regulatory reality in India**: Sending formal letters to banks/cyber cells on someone's behalf without them explicitly controlling the send is a bad idea. Banks want to know the account holder themselves took the action.

- **Proof gates exist for a reason**: The whole system is designed around the user proving they actually sent each level. If we auto-send, that proof becomes meaningless.

**What Unhold actually does instead (and this is the value):**
- Gives perfect letter text + a sealed, verified evidence bundle PDF.
- Tells the user exactly the escalation order (L1 branch first, then prove sent, then L2...).
- Makes it extremely easy to copy the letter and attach the bundle.
- Tracks everything the user did and creates a credible audit trail.
- The user hits send themselves from their own email/post.

This keeps the user in full control and creates a defensible record.

If someone wants a product that auto-sends letters for them, Unhold is not that product (and per our rules, it never will be).

## 14. Additional common objections (short versions)

**"It will get released anyway after 90 days per SOP."**  
Not always automatic. SOP gives timelines for review, but many cases need active follow-up. Some holds extend or require explicit NOC.

**"I don't have time for this — I'm already stressed."**  
Fair. The alternative for many is repeated branch visits, chasing different people, and anxiety for months. Unhold tries to front-load the organization so follow-ups are targeted.

**"Just use RBI Ombudsman directly."**  
Ombudsman is powerful and free (high resolution rate on maintainable complaints), but they usually expect you to have escalated with the bank first. Strong documentation helps your case there too.

**"This only works for SBI."**  
Focus is currently SBI innocent-receiver playbooks (most common complaints), but principles apply more broadly. Scope is limited per BUILD_SPEC.
